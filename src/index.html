About Me

Hello! My name is Tim Sasaki and I’m a licensed clinical social worker with over ten years of experience providing mental health services in English and Spanish in the Bay Area.

My background in community mental health has allowed me to work with a wide range of identities and populations, including: severely emotionally disturbed children, adults with severe mental illness, transitional age LGBTQ youth, immigrants, gay and lesbian adults, transgender and nonbinary adults, and many identities in between.

I help individuals work through challenges like depression, anxiety, life transitions, work stress, relationships, grief and loss, severe mental illness, substance use and addiction, trauma, gender transition, coming out, aging, multiple minority stress, aging in the queer community, and issues related to immigration.

I hold master’s degrees in Social Welfare and Public Health from the University of California Berkeley, and I completed my bachelor’s degree at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. I am a member of the National Association of Social Work, Psychologytoday, and I serve on the Steering Committee for the Coalition of Clinical Social Work at the San Francisco Center for Psychoanalysis.

Services

I provide individual psychotherapy and pre-surgical assessment for gender affirming surgeries at my office in the Mission District of San Francisco, California.

  • Individual Psychotherapy

    I believe the power of therapy lies in improving your relationship to the world, the people close to you, and ultimately to yourself. My approach to therapy is trauma-informed and grounded in psychodynamic and psychoanalytic training. I also use techniques from cognitive behavioral therapy, dialectical behavioral therapy, motivational interviewing, gender affirming therapy, harm reduction, and mindfulness. I welcome any questions you might have about my approach to therapy.

  • Pre-Surgical Assessment for Gender Affirming Surgery

    I am committed to ensuring the assessment process is collaborative, transparent, and guided by your narrative. I have written over 50 assessments (“letters”) for adults pursuing gender affirming surgery. If you are in need of a letter or are thinking about surgery, please feel free to contact me so we can talk more about what to expect of the process.
